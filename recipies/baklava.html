Baklava...

Figure 1: baklava...

Baklava is a delicious and rich dessert that hails from the Middle East and has become popular in many Mediterranean and Balkan cuisines. This pastry is made with layers of thin phyllo dough, filled with chopped nuts (commonly walnuts, pistachios, or a combination), and sweetened with a honey and sugar syrup

Ingredients!

  • 1 package of phyllo dough (about 16 ounces), thawed if frozen
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 cups mixed chopped nuts (walnuts, pistachios, or a combination)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)

Steps, if you wish to dare...

  1. Prepare the Nuts:Combine the chopped nuts with sugar and cinnamon in a bowl. This will be the filling for your baklava.
  2. Prepare the Phyllo Dough:Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  3. Layering:Place one sheet of phyllo dough in the prepared dish and brush it with melted butter.
  4. Repeat:Add another layer of phyllo sheets, brushing each with butter, and topping with the nut mixture. Repeat until you run out of nuts, finishing with a final layer of phyllo sheets on top.
  5. Cutting:Using a sharp knife, carefully cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes. This can be done before or after baking.
  6. Baking:Bake in the preheated oven for about 45-55 minutes or until golden brown and crisp.
  7. Making the Syrup:While the baklava is baking, combine water, sugar, honey, lemon juice, and vanilla extract in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, until slightly thickened.
  8. Finishing Touch:Once the baklava is out of the oven, immediately pour the warm syrup evenly over the hot pastry. Allow the baklava to absorb the syrup and cool completely before serving.
  9. Serve and Enjoy:Baklava is best when allowed to sit for a few hours or overnight, allowing the flavors to meld. Serve the delicious squares and savor the sweet, nutty layers.
